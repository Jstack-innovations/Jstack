Digital Education System – Professional Conversation Flow

This document represents the recommended conversation structure when presenting the Digital School Portal and Tech Capability Program to schools, students, and parents.

1. Conversation: Developer → School Management

You:
I operate a structured digital education system designed to modernize school operations and introduce controlled technical capability to students.
You:
The system includes a school portal, CBT exams and quizzes, academic records, and a structured tech skills exposure program.
(Pause. Allow the school to ask questions.)

2. Explaining the Tech Skills Program

School:
What does the tech program involve?
You:
I offer multiple tech stacks such as web development, digital skills, and software fundamentals. Each school selects one stack only.
You:
Training delivered inside the school is foundation-level. It provides exposure and understanding, not specialization.

3. Defining Clear Boundaries

You:
My full professional training program is not sellable or hireable by any single institution.
You:
Schools participate through structured partnerships. Depth and mastery are handled independently by my teams.

4. Student Advancement Path

School:
How do interested students become professionals?
You:
Students who want professional-level competence enroll in my external mastery program.
You:
Under my teams, students can reach industry-ready capability within six to twelve months, depending on commitment.

5. Payment & Revenue Structure

You:
The school pays for the portal and the in-school exposure program.
You:
Parents pay directly for professional training. The school earns a commission for students enrolled through them.

6. Hiring Clarification

School:
Can we hire you fully?
You:
Hiring applies only to one selected tech stack and is part-time. The full professional program remains independent and non-negotiable.

7. Communication to Students

You:
What you are learning here is the foundation for this tech stack.
You:
Professional mastery happens through a separate program. Details are available through the school.

8. Final Positioning

You:
This is not a tutoring arrangement. It is a structured digital capability partnership.
You:
The school gains prestige, efficiency, and revenue. I retain program control, quality, and scalability.
